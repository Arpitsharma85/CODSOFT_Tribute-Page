Bhagat Singh

1907 - 1931

The Immortal Revolutionary

Bhagat Singh Sandhu 28 September 1907 – 23 March 1931) was an fearless freedom fighter, was a symbol of courage, sacrifice, and patriotism. Born on September 28, 1907, in Banga, Punjab, he dedicated his life to the fight against British rule in India. Inspired by the sacrifices of great revolutionaries, he joined the freedom movement at a young age and became a prominent member of the Hindustan Socialist Republican Association (HSRA). His famous slogan, "Inquilab Zindabad" (Long Live the Revolution), continues to ignite the spirit of patriotism in millions. Bhagat Singh, along with Rajguru and Sukhdev, was martyred on March 23, 1931, at the young age of 23, leaving behind a legacy of bravery and selflessness. His unwavering commitment to the nation and his vision of a free and just India make him an eternal inspiration for generations to come.



About The Legend

  • Born into a Freedom Fighter’s Family – Bhagat Singh was born on September 28, 1907, in a Sikh family with strong patriotic values. His father and uncles were also involved in India’s freedom struggle.
  • Jallianwala Bagh Massacre's Impact – The brutal killing of innocent people in 1919 at Jallianwala Bagh deeply affected him, fueling his desire to fight against British rule.
  • Fearless and Brave – He showed extraordinary courage, never fearing death, and stood strong against the British Empire with unshakable determination.
  • Sharp Intelligence and Ideology – Bhagat Singh was a deep thinker who read books on socialism, revolution, and justice. His ideas went beyond independence—he envisioned a just and equal society.
  • Lala Lajpat Rai’s Revenge – To avenge the death of Lala Lajpat Rai, who was injured in a police lathi charge, Bhagat Singh and his associates killed British officer J.P. Saunders in 1928.
  • Central Legislative Assembly Bombing – In 1929, Bhagat Singh and Batukeshwar Dutt threw non-lethal bombs in the Central Legislative Assembly to protest against oppressive British laws and got arrested willingly.
  • Jail Hunger Strike & Leadership – He led a 116-day hunger strike in jail, demanding better conditions for Indian prisoners. His leadership inspired many revolutionaries.
  • Execution on March 23, 1931 – Bhagat Singh, Rajguru, and Sukhdev were sentenced to death and hanged in Lahore Central Jail. His last words were: "Down with British imperialism!"
  • Legacy and Eternal Inspiration – His famous slogan "Inquilab Zindabad" (Long Live the Revolution) still echoes in India. His qualities of courage, intelligence, patriotism, and selflessness make him an everlasting inspiration.
